we were here first

a short & slightly smug history of the creative department of the internet.

there's a particular feeling — you know the one — of watching something you made five years ago get repackaged, re-skinned, and re-announced as if it were new. the web is a very long memory with a very short attention span, and 247420 has been, quietly, around for most of it.

this isn't a victory lap. it's a map. because if you're showing up to the party now, you should at least know who set the table.

we don't know which ideas become the future. we just know most of them come from the same few rooms.

the rooms have moved — from irc to forums to discord to whatever-this-is-now — but the pattern is consistent. a handful of people, each a little too weird to hold a normal job, trading prototypes at 2am, throwing away 90% of them, shipping the 10% that's too strange to ignore. that's the collective. that's always been the collective. adaptogen, gm, zellous, the long list of things you've never heard of — all came out of the same 2am.

so what's the actual point

the point is: stop asking permission. ship the rough draft. document honestly. humor is load-bearing. and if you're reading this thinking "that's obvious" — good. you're home.
